03 · SAP RE-FX
Real Estate Management & IFRS 16 Compliance
EN  English
Lease portfolios have quietly become one of the most complex compliance challenges on corporate balance sheets. IFRS 16 and ASC 842 require organizations to recognize virtually all leases as assets and liabilities  a change that impacts reported leverage, EBITDA, and covenant calculations across industries. SAP RE-FX provides the architectural foundation to manage this complexity at scale: automated amortization schedules, parallel accounting for different reporting standards, and full audit trails for every contractual event. Beyond compliance, the module is evolving to address ESG imperatives  tracking energy consumption and carbon footprint at the asset level to meet CSRD reporting obligations. IoT integration is extending visibility further, connecting building management systems directly to SAP for real-time facility data. Companies with large lease or property portfolios that treat RE-FX as a strategic system  not merely a compliance tool  gain material advantages in portfolio optimization and cost control.

Central Finance extends this architecture to heterogeneous environments  organizations running multiple ERP systems can centralize financial data into a single S/4HANA hub without requiring subsidiary system migrations. Beyond compliance, Turkish localization requires understanding how local banking infrastructure, tax calendars, and regulatory reporting cycles interact with SAP's global architecture. This is not configuration work  it is financial architecture work.
